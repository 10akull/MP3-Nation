ABOUT

"Whatever It Takes" is a song by American rock band Imagine Dragons. The song was released on May 8, 2017, as a promotional single through Kidinakorner and Interscope. It later became the third single from the band's third studio album, Evolve, on October 6, 2017. It was also the official theme song for WWE's PPV event Battleground, in addition to its inclusion in the video game Madden NFL 18. The song will be sent to US contemporary hit radio on February 13, 2018.

Background

The song was announced alongside the name and album art of Evolve via Twitter on May 9, 2017.

MUSIC VIDEO

A music video for the song was released on October 12, 2017. It was directed by Matt Eastin and Aaron Hymes. Eastin had also previously directed the music videos for Imagine Dragon's songs "On Top of the World", "Roots", and "Believer". The video begins with lead singer Dan Reynolds swimming through a room filled with water. It then cuts to a scene in which Reynolds and the band are playing in the same room, however it is not filled with water in this scene. As they perform the chorus of the song, the roof crumbles and rain stars pouring in. Throughout the song the rain persists and the room eventually fills completely with water. However the band still plays. As Reynolds sings, objects such as boxing gloves and keys float by him along with two people, seemingly mermaids tugging at him from both sides. Before the final chorus the music stops and it shows the band lifelessly floating beneath the water. When they break into song again, the stage has completely changed as the room is no longer underwater, but is now on fire as the band plays in front of it. As the song ends, everything is on fire and the music stops.
